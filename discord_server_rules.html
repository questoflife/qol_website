Discord Server Rules

Our top priority is to create a supportive space for everyone in the community. This applies to both groups and the server as a whole. We will therefore be relatively strict with enforcing rules and recommend you read the rules to avoid mutes and/or bans. Moderators will do their best to be compassionate and to understand your intentions when making decisions, but they will have final say in all interpretations.

1. Discord ToS & Legal

Please follow Discord’s Terms of Service and Community Guidelines at all times. Do not engage in any illegal activity.

2. Age Restriction

You must be at least 21 years old to participate in this server.

3. NSFW Content

All NSFW content is strictly prohibited, including, but not limited to, pornography, gore, and unsettling content.

4. Hate

Absolutely no hate speech or discrimination based on race, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, national origin, age, ancestry, disability, or any anything else. We welcome everyone here.

5. Abstract Discussions on Politics, Religion, History, Philosophy

Please refrain from discussing general topics if the aim isn’t to support a specific individual. It is okay to talk about how a political situation is affecting you personally, but please avoid abstract discussions about the listed topics

6. Spam

Do not spam, spam ping, mass mention, or chat flood in this server. Do not DM other members without asking for permission within the server first. Do not shout or scream in voice channels. Do not post excessive or out of context images, GIFs and links. Avoid sending lots of short messages when they can be condensed into a longer sentence.

7. Do Not Generalize

Avoid making generalizations about groups of people.

8. Profile

We require all member profiles to be appropriate. No content that would violate other rules (such as NSFW or hate content) is allowed in your username, nickname, profile picture, banner, status, and about me.

9. Hacking/Doxing

Do not threaten or attempt to hack and/or dox anyone in this server. Violations will be reported to Discord staff immediately.

10. Raid Threats

Do not threaten or attempt to raid the server, even as a joke. Violations will be reported to Discord staff. If you notice any raid threats or attempts against the server, contact a @Moderator immediately.

11. Personal Information

Do not share your or anyone else’s personal information in this server.

12. Channel Use

Keep conversation relevant to channels.

13. Loopholes

Do not attempt to find and/or use loopholes in the rules. Moderators may intervene if they see behavior they deem inappropriate for the server, even if it isn’t explicitly mentioned in the rules. To report mod abuse, please submit a ticket in #support-desk.

14. Professional Advice

Do not request or give professional advice of any kind in this server.

15. Substance Use

Do not encourage drug/alcohol or other substance use. Do not use the server if you are under the influence of substances. The only acceptable context to discuss substances is talking about addiction and how it’s affecting your life.

16. Medical Topics

Do not discuss medication or supplement use. Do not ask for or give medical advice of any kind. Do not try to diagnose each other. What you can do is talk about how you’re affected by medical conditions.

17. Language

All messages must be in English only.

18. Be respectful to others

Do not insult, judge, invalidate or dismiss others or their experiences. Do not use slurs or other derogatory language. This extends to memes, GIFs, videos and other content. Do not engage in ‘edgy’ behavior. ‘It was just a joke’ will not be considered a valid excuse for problematic behavior.

19. Do not argue

If a conversation becomes heated and all parties wish to continue, please move to DMs.

20. Do not self promote
Communication Guidelines

Here are some guidelines designed to help us all build a better and more supportive community.

  1. Use the communication skills we learn in group: reflective listening, asking open-ended questions, relating
  2. Report rule violations by DMing a moderator
  3. If you’re joining a voice call, listen first to figure out what people are talking about before jumping in.
  4. Be patient with people. Many of us aren't going to be native speakers or may need extra time to express themselves.
  5. Use spoiler tags if you’re going to talk about potentially triggering topics. You can do this by typing || Insert your message here ||
  6. Do not give advice unless explicitly asked to do so.
  7. Respect others’ agency and boundaries. If they don’t want to talk about something, don’t push it. Ask permission before sending DMs and if they decline, respect their decision.
  8. Do not flirt with other members.

Open-Ended Questions

Any questions that provoke thought, inviting a paragraph answer rather than a single word. Basically, not yes/no or multiple choice questions.

Why?

  • Encourage further reflection
  • Find out more about what someone's going through
  • Move the conversation forward

How?

  • Be curious - what would you like to know more about?
  • Clarify statements - is there anything ambiguous or confusing? Ask about it! Chances are, someone else in the group is confused too. Sometimes even the person sharing won't be aware of something inconsistent. Asking for clarification is what often leads to great insights.
  • Are you making assumptions about anything? Ask a question to find out what's actually going on!

Examples

  • What do you think about that?
  • How did you learn that...?
  • How do you feel about that?

Reflective Listening

Reflective listening is any answer to 'What are you hearing the other person say?'. Could be a summary, a direct quote, some key highlights, or even sharing how you got a bit lost in thought and only caught the last sentence of what someone was saying...

Why?

  • Check if you're understanding what the other person is sharing
  • Give them an easy way to clarify misunderstandings
  • Help someone feel heard and understood
  • Invite someone to share more. When you reflect something back to another person, generally they will naturally share more and go more in depth.
  • Enable new insights. Hearing our words come out of someone else's mouth often makes them land in a different way and we notice things in our own thinking we otherwise wouldn't. You don't have to provide the actual insight - just tell someone what you're hearing and let them connect the dots.

Examples

  • I'm hearing that...
  • It sounds like you...
  • That sounds exhausting!

Relating Your Experience

Sometimes another group member will share something you relate to - consider sharing your experience. If you're unsure about whether they'd like to hear it or rather keep the focus on them, you can also ask them.

Why?

  • Creates a sense of 'you're not alone in this'
  • Especially useful if someone's feelings of judgment or shame associated with their experience
  • Helps you connect with others in the group

Examples

  • Me too!
  • I feel like I can relate a lot. Would you be interested in hearing my experience?
